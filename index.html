Before You Eat: 5 Korean Dining Rules

1. "Banchan" & Refills
Small side dishes (Banchan) are served with your meal. Refills are often available upon polite request ("Deo Ju-se-yo"). However, this does not mean "All-You-Can-Eat" for the main dish. It is polite to finish what is served before asking for more. Excessive food waste is frowned upon, so please order responsibly.


2. "Water is Self" (Location & Manners)
If you see a sign saying "Water is Self," look for a water purifier or a stainless steel cup sterilizer nearby. While water is free, please be respectful‚Äîuse only the cups you need and don't fill up large personal bottles to take home.


3. Chopsticks and Spoons
Korea uniquely uses metal chopsticks and a long spoon. The rule is simple: use the spoon for rice and soups, and chopsticks for side dishes. It‚Äôs clean, efficient, and part of the experience.


4. NO Tipping (It's the Law)
Tipping is NOT required and is effectively illegal. The price on the menu includes VAT and service charges. Offering a tip might even be refused or considered awkward. A warm "Kamsahamnida" (Thank you) is the best way to show appreciation.


5. The "Call Button" Etiquette
If you see a button on the table, press it once and wait patiently. It calls the server ("Ding-Dong"). If they don't arrive during busy hours, it is okay to press again or raise your hand. Important: Do not press the button repeatedly in quick succession; this is considered rude.
